Overview

Smart Cart simplifies the process of planning meals and building grocery lists. Users create meals by adding ingredients with structured quantity and unit support, and the app automatically aggregates shared ingredients across recipes into a single consolidated shopping list.

It also integrates location-aware store discovery to help users find nearby grocery options, reducing friction between planning meals and completing a shopping trip.

How it works

Smart Cart organizes the cooking workflow into three connected steps: meal planning, ingredient normalization, and grocery aggregation. Users create meals by adding ingredients with selectable measurement units — stored persistently and reused across the app.

Ingredients from multiple meals are automatically merged into a unified grocery list, where users can inspect quantities, track usage across recipes, and adjust measurement formats. Map-based store discovery is integrated directly into the shopping workflow.

Technical highlights

Built with Flutter and Isar for persistent local storage, supporting structured ingredient tracking and user unit preferences. The app handles asynchronous loading of large grocery store map datasets and includes GPS-based store lookup via OpenStreetMap. Additional features include gesture-based navigation, unit conversion, and internationalization-ready quantity handling.

Usability evaluation

Evaluated using System Usability Scale (SUS) testing with nine participants. Overall score: 84.2 — usability 88.9, learnability 83.0. Users were able to navigate the meal planning workflow quickly and without confusion.
